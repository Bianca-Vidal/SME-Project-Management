Waterfall

The project management (PM) waterfall method is the preferred methodology for large scale projects such used in the construction industry. This is largely linked to how tightly it monitors every stage of the project. However, the Waterfall Method could be advantageous to SME's. Expert project Manager O'Callaghan stated that SME's who have a fixed project end would benefit largely from using this methodology in their business, (O'Callaghan reported in Tamang, 2018). Such businesses would be those who deliver a service such as those consultation service, networking of a small business, installation of security devices in a shop etc..

The Waterfall Method has a systematic procedure of working through a project listed below;

  1. Analysis
  2. Design
  3. Construction
  4. Testing
  5. Support

If your business is a service end business please review the advantages and disadvantages of incorporating the Waterfall (PMM) within your business.




Advantages
  • Clear understanding of objectives
  • Accurate end result
  • Measurable progress

Disadvantages
  • Time consuming for discovering the requirements for the business
  • Restrictive to changes after requirements has been collected
  • High Cost





Examples of service based SME businesses are as follows;
  • Consultation Business
  • Installation Business
  • Training
  • Solicitor/ Legal Advice
  • Catering Business
  • Photography Business
  • Bookeeping/ Financial Services
  • Plumbing Business
  • Landscaping Services
All of which deliver a service to the end user/ customer.
